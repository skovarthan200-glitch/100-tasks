Building a Scalable Typography System

A well-designed type system is the foundation of any great interface. Learn how to create one that scales across your entire product.

Sarah Chen January 15, 2026

Typography is more than just picking fonts. It's about creating a system that communicates hierarchy, establishes rhythm, and guides users through your content. When done right, it becomes invisible—users focus on your message, not your design.

Start with a Modular Scale

The Major Third scale (1.250) provides enough contrast between levels while keeping the system manageable. Each step is 1.25x larger than the previous, creating a harmonious progression.

Define Your Base Size

Most interfaces use 16px as the base. This ensures readability across devices and meets accessibility standards. From there, scale up for headings and down for captions.

Pro tip: Test your type scale at different viewport sizes. What looks good on desktop might feel cramped on mobile.
